# Maroon Villages — Research Notes

## Status: DRAFT — for review before publication

Compiled by web scraping Wikipedia, Minority Rights Group, Cultural Survival, Encyclopedia.com,
Mapcarta, Joshua Project, UNDP documentation, and academic sources. March 2026.

-----

## What this list covers

Villages and settlements of the six Maroon groups in Suriname and French Guiana (Guyane):

- **Ndyuka** (Aukan / Okanisi) — eastern Suriname and NE French Guiana
- **Saramaka** (Saamaka) — upper Suriname River, central Suriname
- **Matawai** — Saramacca River tributaries, central Suriname
- **Paramaka** (Paamaka / Paramaccan) — Marowijne/Lawa basin, NE Suriname and W French Guiana
- **Aluku** (Boni) — Lawa River, mainly French Guiana
- **Kwinti** — Coppename River, central-west Suriname

-----

## What is NOT yet in this list — known gaps

### Saramaka — major gap

The Wikipedia article on the Upper Suriname River lists roughly 50+ villages by name along the river
and its tributaries (Gran Rio, Pikin Rio). I have extracted the named locations but many of these are
approximate placeholders — coordinates are estimated from river geography, not GPS-verified.

**The Wikipedia article explicitly lists these village clusters/names along the river:**
Pikinpada · Bakaaboto, Duwatra & Bekiokondre · Gingiston, Pambo Oko & Biudumatu ·
Kaajapati & Salubanga · Jawjaw & Isadou · Lespansi · Adawai & Malroseekondre ·
Gunsi, Ferulasi, Tang Luku, Anaula & Kninipaati · Djemongo & Nazaleti · Gujaba & Beguba ·
Debikè · Botopasi · Kambalua & Koonoo · Dan & Danpaati · Padalafanti & Umansanti ·
Malobi · Masiakriki & Masiakrikipaati · Heikununu · Tumaripa · Semoisi ·
Pingpe, Mambijai & Daume · Akwaukondre, Gran Slee, Bofokule & Apiapaati ·
Dangogo 1 & 2 · Kumbasula · Kampu · Begoon, Ligorio & Moitori · Stonhuku ·
Awaradam (and Sentiadam) · Bendiwata & Kuututen · Langu · Goddo · Djumu / Soolan

Several of these (e.g. Malroseekondre, Umansanti, Masiakriki, Mambijai, Daume, Bekiokondre,
Pambo Oko, Biudumatu, Ferulasi, Tang Luku, Kninipaati, Bofokule, Apiapaati, Ligorio, Moitori,
Kuututen, Sentiadam, Salubanga, Duwatra) have NOT been given individual entries yet because
their coordinates cannot be reliably estimated without a detailed river map.
These need to be added in a second pass using GIS data or OpenStreetMap.

### Ndyuka — approximately 49 villages in Tapanahony resort alone

The Wikipedia article on Tapanahony states the resort has “about 49 Ndyuka villages and 14
indigenous villages.” Only a handful are individually named in available web sources. A full
Ndyuka village list requires:

- Surinamese government planning documents (Planbureau Suriname — Districts 2009-2013)
- Field research / Medische Zending health post data
- Dutch academic literature (Thoden van Velzen, Kobben, etc.)

Known Ndyuka villages that need to be added when more data is available:
Poeloegoedoe (historical, confluence Tapanahoni-Lawa) · Benzdorp · Godo Olo ·
Koffiekamp (Ndyuka area) · Cotica River villages (name lost during 1986 war) ·
Multiple unnamed Cottica region villages razed in 1986

### Matawai — approximately 13–19 villages

Wikipedia (Matawai people article) states “there were 19 villages controlled by the Matawai;
however, the Surinamese Interior War caused the abandonment of six villages.”
Only the granman seat (Poesoegroenoe) and the main village (Nieuw Jacobkondre) have been
confidently identified. ~10 more Matawai villages on the upper Saramacca River need to be added.
ACT-Guianas references “about ten Matawai villages on the upper Saramacca River” in their
December 2025 development plans report.

### Paramaka — 13 villages in Suriname + Providence in French Guiana

Wikipedia (Paramaccan) states the tribe controls 13 villages. Only 6 have been identified so far:
Langatabiki · Snesiekondre · Nason · Gakaba · Lokaloka · Stoelmanseiland · Providence (FG)
The remaining ~7 Paramaka villages are not individually named in available English-language sources.

### Aluku — French Guiana villages

The main Aluku settlements in the Maripasoula and Papaïchton communes have been captured.
However, smaller traditional villages and hamlets along the upper Lawa and Maroni rivers are
not fully documented in English-language web sources. French-language academic literature
(Jean Hurault, Kenneth Bilby) would add significantly more detail.

### French Guiana Ndyuka and Paramaka settlements

The French Guiana Maroons report (2021 PDF) references Ndyuka villages in French Guiana
“mainly found in the area around the confluence of the Lawa and Tapanahoni.”
These cross-border settlements (Sangamangsoesa to Gaanboli area) are partially captured but
their exact names and coordinates have not been individually resolved.
Grand-Santi has a largely Ndyuka population but functions as a French administrative commune.

The Dutch colonial spelling (oe = u, j = y) creates systematic variation:

- Dutch: Drietabbetje → Ndyuka: Diitabiki
- Dutch: Djumu → Standard: Djumu
- Dutch: Poesoegroenoe → Saramaccan: Pusugrunu
  All three spellings of any given village may appear in different sources.

## Coordinate confidence levels

**HIGH** — coordinates sourced from Wikipedia with specific geographic description,
or from OpenStreetMap/Mapcarta with referenced location. Likely accurate to <2km.

**MEDIUM** — coordinates estimated from river geography and regional description.
Village is known to exist on a specific river section but exact position estimated.
Likely accurate to 5–15km. Needs GPS verification.

**LOW** — village name confirmed in text sources but location is approximate or inferred.
Coordinates are indicative only. Needs independent verification before use in mapping.

## Recommended next steps for improving completeness

1. **Dutch-language sources**: Surinamese government planning documents (Planbureau Suriname),
   particularly the Districts tables 2009-2013 which list village populations by resort.
   These would substantially fill the Ndyuka and Saramaka gaps.
1. **Medische Zending (Medical Mission) map**: The Wikipedia article on Upper Suriname River
   cites a Google Maps layer from Medische Zending showing all medical post villages.
   This would add ~15 confirmed Upper Suriname River village locations with coordinates.
   URL: https://www.google.com/maps/d/viewer?mid=1C8qfmd6C6Kdo-rubY7RncptYfOc
1. **OpenStreetMap**: Query the OSM database for nodes tagged as villages in Sipaliwini District
   with Maroon group associations. This would confirm coordinates for medium-confidence entries.
1. **French Guiana**: French academic sources (Jean Hurault, Kenneth Bilby) document Aluku
   villages in detail; these are not well represented in English-language web sources.
1. **IACtHR Saramaka case documents**: The 2007 judgement and supporting case files list
   the 12 Saramaka clans and 60+ villages in the Upper Suriname River area. These legal
   documents are publicly available and would be the most authoritative source for the
   Saramaka village list.
