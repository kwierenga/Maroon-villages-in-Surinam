Wan Tori fu Feti & Ferteri

Den Marron fu Sranan — pikin-pikin fu den Afrikanen di lobi abra srefiworku, feti agepi kolonial legersi, en bilt frei seyten ini busi — e sori wan fu den moro bijsonkre tori fu sma-ferteri ini den Amerika. Den feti fu sikhesrefi no klari na 1760. A e go hay tide.

Siksi Pipel Na Liba Sei

Ndyuka, Saramaka, Matawai, Paramaka, Aluku, nanga Kwinti — siksi aparte Marron pipel, drape wan abi na ini en eigi tongo, besturu, nanga kulturu, e libi na liba sei fu Sranan. Ala siksi ben fesi — en sori dati den e libi — kolonial woro, moderen insei-woro, furu, nanga a dreygi fu hari den gron.

Winti, Ati-Worku & Libi Kulturu

Fu Winti — wan meskibakra fu Wesi- nanga Sentru-Afrika religie — teki go na ingi-kapwerk, moro-moro wewi tekstil, nanga kreol tongo di no wan trawan e taki na ini a heri wrele, Marron kulturu-opo na wan fu den moro riku ini den Afrikaanse diaspora.

Bun Kon — Wan Tori fu Ferteri & Libi Abra

The Surinamese Maroons — also known as Businenge or Bushinengue, meaning "black people of the forest" — are the descendants of Africans who escaped enslavement on Dutch colonial plantations during the 17th and 18th centuries. They fled into the interior rainforest, regrouped into small bands, and began forging a viable existence in an inhospitable environment. This daunting challenge was made more difficult still by the colonial government's persistent efforts to eliminate them — reserving special punishments for recaptured people: hamstringing, amputation of limbs, and deaths by torture. The Maroons survived all of it.

Bijna wan hondert yari den feti — den Marron ben e yepi den kolonial soldati doro guerrilla wisi, den ben e sribi na baka houtun weri nanga futu-fal ini priti busi — te di den Olanse, mati nanga den kosto fu moni nanga libi, kon na taletapu. A libi fu Marron kondre ben e sori ala srefiworku-man taki frei e de posibel. Na 1760 den Ndyuka ben wiki wan fredi-traktat; na 1762 den Saramaka; na 1767 den Matawai. Den Marron ben libi hondert yari frei bifesi srefiworku ben anbegi na Sranan na 1863.

A tori fu feti no klari nanga inkorosenti. Kolonial woro, teki gron, naturu brokodoro, insei-woro, nanga furu ben meki maka na ala kondre fu Marron tori — en e go hay tide. Den kulturu e si bigi ogri taki na wan fu den moro bun bewari stuku fu Afrika kulturu-grontapu buten Afrika. A website de fu dokumenteri a kulturu-grontapu dati nanga a feti di e go hay.

10 Oktober na a Nasronal Dey fu den Marron na Sranan — fu memre a ferste fredi-traktat, sineteri na dati dey na 1760 nanga den Ndyuka pipel.

Wan Olutu fu Feti & Libi Abra

The history of the Surinamese Maroons is not a relic of the past. It is a continuous story of people who refused enslavement, built free societies against overwhelming force, survived colonial warfare and a modern civil war, and continue today to fight for the recognition of rights their ancestors won three centuries ago. Each era demanded — and received — the same determined response.

1650s–1700sOrigins
Escape & Foundation

Flight from the Plantations

Enslaved Africans — brought to Suriname from the Gold Coast, West Central Africa, the Windward Coast, and the Bight of Benin — began escaping Dutch colonial plantations from the mid-17th century. Individually, in small groups, and through collective rebellions, they fled into the dense interior rainforest. The mere existence of Maroon villages in the forest was living proof to enslaved people on the plantations that an alternative to slavery was possible.

1700s–1750sResistance
Guerrilla War

Half a Century of Armed Resistance

The Dutch mounted repeated military expeditions into the interior to recapture escaped slaves and destroy Maroon villages — one in 1730 included 50 citizens, 200 enslaved workers, and 15 Indigenous auxiliaries; another in 1743 required 60 canoes. The Maroons, protected by swamps and dense forest, fortified their settlements with wooden palisades and pitfalls, and became expert at guerrilla tactics. Most expeditions were fruitless and enormously costly — each exceeding 100,000 guilders. By the late 1740s the colony was financially exhausted and sued for peace.

1760Ndyuka Treaty
Fredi-Traktat

10 October 1760 — The First Peace Treaty

On 10 October 1760, the Ndyuka became the first Maroon group to sign a formal peace treaty with the Dutch colonial authority — drafted in part with assistance from Adyáko Benti Basiton, a Maroon negotiator who had access to the Jamaican peace treaties of 1739–1740. The treaty recognised Ndyuka freedom and territorial rights along the upper Tapanahoni River basin, the right to bear arms, and self-governance under a granman. This date is now the national Day of the Maroons in Suriname, observed since 1974. The Saramaka followed in 1762, the Matawai in 1767, and the Kwinti — last — in 1887.

1760s–1863100 Years Free
Autonomy

A Century of Freedom Before Abolition

The Maroons had won their freedom nearly a century before slavery was finally abolished throughout Suriname in 1863. In that time, they built culturally rich, politically autonomous communities along the interior rivers. The six groups developed distinct languages, art traditions, governance systems, and spiritual practices. Economically, they became indispensable as boatmen, river guides, and cargo carriers for colonial and, later, foreign companies — effectively controlling river transport in both Suriname and French Guiana for decades.

1880s–1930sGold Rush Era
Economy

River Masters of the Interior

When rich gold deposits were discovered in the hinterland in the late 1880s, Ndyuka and Saramaka men abandoned the lumber trade to monopolise river transport, paddling dugout canoes loaded with miners and equipment through rapids and shallow waterways that outsiders could not navigate. By 1900–1925 this had become a peak of Maroon economic power. When the balata rubber company closed in 1931, roughly one-third of its more than 1,000 laid-off workers were Maroon — indicating how deeply Maroon labour had become embedded in the colonial economy while communities maintained their interior villages.

1975Independence
Post-Colonial

Independence — Old Treaties Abrogated

When Suriname gained independence from the Netherlands in 1975, the colonial-era peace treaties that had guaranteed Maroon territorial autonomy, self-governance, and cultural freedom were effectively abrogated. The new state, dominated by urban Creole political elites, pursued policies to resettle Maroon communities and integrate them into national life — directly threatening the sovereignty that their ancestors had fought and died to secure. Resentment deepened through the early 1980s under the military dictatorship of Dési Bouterse.

1986–1992Interior War
Civil War

The Surinamese Interior War — History Repeating

On 22 July 1986, Ronnie Brunswijk — a Ndyuka Maroon and former bodyguard of Bouterse — launched the Jungle Commando rebellion, demanding democratic rights, civil liberties, and economic development for the marginalised Maroon population. The military response was devastating: on 29 November 1986, soldiers attacked the Ndyuka village of Moiwana, massacring more than 40 people — mostly women and children — and burning the village to the ground. An estimated 7,000–10,000 Maroons fled as refugees to French Guiana. Villages across eastern Suriname were razed; Maroons in Paramaribo were arrested and murdered in night raids. A peace treaty was finally signed on 8 August 1992.

2005–2007Justice
Human Rights

Inter-American Court Rulings: Accountability & Land Rights

In June 2005, the Inter-American Court of Human Rights found Suriname guilty of human rights violations for the Moiwana massacre, ordering reparations to survivors and a public state apology to the Ndyuka people. In 2007, a landmark ruling in Saramaka People v. Suriname determined that the Saramaka constitute a tribal people with legally protected territorial rights over lands they have historically occupied — representing a landmark decision for Maroon and Indigenous peoples worldwide. The Surinamese government has been slow to implement both rulings fully.

1990s–2010sResource Threat
Ongoing Threat

Logging, Mining & Land Concessions

After the civil war, the Surinamese government granted large timber and mining concessions to foreign multinationals — Chinese, Indonesian, Malaysian, and others — in traditional Maroon territory, without consulting Maroon authorities. Chinese timber companies, newly active after a domestic logging ban, began operating directly on Saramaka lands. Meanwhile, a gold rush drew large numbers of Brazilian garimpeiros into the interior. A majority of Ndyuka men and some Ndyuka women became involved in small-scale gold mining — creating income but also environmental damage and social disruption within their own communities.

2020–TodayPresent
Today

A Maroon Vice President — and Unfinished Business

On 16 July 2020, Ronnie Brunswijk — once a rebel commander with a bounty on his head — was inaugurated as Vice President of Suriname, the first Maroon to hold that office. His Abop Party had won the 2020 elections in coalition. Yet unresolved land rights claims persist. According to the 2012 census, 117,567 people — 21.7% of Suriname's population — identify as Maroon, making them the country's second largest ethnic group. Many now live in Paramaribo and in the Netherlands. The fight for full territorial recognition, protection from mining and logging, and equality of access to education and healthcare continues.

A Feti E Go Hay — Tide

Gron-Reti & Territoriale Sikhesrefi

Maski a bigi IACtHR beslissing fu 2007 di piki a Saramaka gron-reti, Sranan no meki wan heri wet di e piki den kolektieve gron-reti fu Marron nanga Indigenous pipel. A staat e tan gi houtkap nanga minari konsesies na owru Marron grontapu sonder a vrij, fosi nanga informeer toestemming di internationaal wetigi e fereis. Den Saramaka kisi soms betaling nanga ontwikkeling fonds, ma a ondro-ligging wetigi kader fu gron-piki e tan no klari fu ala siksi grupu. Feti fu reti ben e go doro nasronal kommissies (1992, 1996, 2000, 2006, 2013) nanga internationaal hof. Na 1976, a ferste gron-reti mars waka 150 kilometer fu Albina go na Paramaribo nanga a leus: "Gron-reti na mensenrechten." Dati leus e tan evenzo presi tide taki fosi yari pasa.

Gowtu Minari — Moni nanga Milieu Kosto

A Sranan busi abi wan fu den moro gowtu-riku presi na heri wreld. Fu a mindri fu den 1980 yari, wan gowtu-rush bringi telori minari sma. Braziliaans garimpeiros e meki bijna dri-kwart fu den pikin-pikin minari; Marron manpikin — fu de Ndyuka, ma tu Paramaka nanga Matawai — e meki a moro biksi pisi fu den libi-abra. Gowtu gi moni na presi pe no abi formele arbeidsmarkt, ma nanga sweri kosto. Mercuriu, di den e yusri ini amalgamatie, e vervuil liba nanga fisi — a mero-moro proteïne-bron fu busi kondre. Bijna 40–50 ton mercuriu e lasi ini Sranan liba ala yari fu pikin-pikin minari. Boskap, erosie, nanga a kon fu minari kamp e brokodey owru kondre besturu-strukturen. Den Marron e libi ini wan tegenstrijdig situasie: den bede abi voordeel fu nanga kisi skada fu a extractie fu sani fu den eigi owru-nen grontapu.

Gezonheid — Medische Zending nanga a Busi

Busi gezonheid zorg e gi bijna olo doro a Medische Zending (MZ), wan niet-gouvernementeel organisatie di e besturu gezonheid posti ini bijna 64 Marron kondre. Sonder a MZ, a moro biksi pisi fu busi kondre no abi entrée na basiszorg. Moeder nanga pikin sterfte na a busi e tan moro hogi dan a nasronal gemiddelde. Malaria na endemisch. Mercuriu vergiftiging fu gowtu minari na wan dokumenteri ma no-gemeten gezonheid last. A MZ e wroko ondro chronische financieel beperkingen nanga e libi moro-moro na vliegende verpleegkundigen nanga bezoekende dokters di e tran doro pikin vliegtuig nanga boto. A Sranan Ministerie fu Volksgezondheid no abi operationeel aanwezigheid na a mero biksi pisi fu busi kondre. Entrée na tweedelijns nanga specialist zorg e fereis wan reis go na Paramaribo — wan tocht fu foto dey fu den moro ferwei kondre.

Onderwijs — Gaten nanga Vooruitgang

Staat basisscholen kon na a mero biksi pisi fu Marron busi kondre pas na den 1960 yari. Ini a 1986–1992 Binnenlandse Woro, scholen stop wroko na bigi pisi fu den Cottica, Marowijne, nanga Tapanahony regio nanga ben pisi-pisi bilt baka na 1992. Tide, voortgezet onderwijs na bijna afwezig ini a busi — pikinso di klari a basisschool mu lobi go na Paramaribo nanga sisei foto fu leri verder, wan prosess di e skeidi den fu den famiri nanga kondre, fu de formeel. Lees nanga skrifi nanga a busi e tan na ondro a nasronal gemiddelde. Organisaties nanga NIKOS nanga internationaal NGO e sribi fasi kondre onderwijs-initiatieven. A Sranan besturu meki toezeggingen fu spreidi busi onderwijs, ma a implementasie ben tra nanga no-gelijk.

Bevolking, Diaspora & Foto Migratie

A 2012 Census dokumenteri 117.567 sikhesrefi-piki Marron na Sranan — 21,7% fu a nasronal bevolking, e meki den a tweede moro bigi etnische grupu fu a kondre. Bigi nommer e libi buten owru grontapu: estimation e suggereri moro fu 40.000 Marron na Paramaribo, moro fu 11.000 na Olanda, nanga groeiende kondre na Frans Guyana, Frankryk, nanga a VS. Foto migratie ben sneller baka a 1986–1992 Binnenlandse Woro nanga e tan baka den jongu sma e suku wroko nanga onderwijs. A diaspora e aktief kibri kulturu eygi fesi doro a Winti religie, kreol tongo, libi-loop seremonie, nanga a yari-yari memre fu a Dey fu den Marron na 10 Oktober. Kondre organisaties na Olanda nanga Frankryk kon bigi ferbiten fu Marron gron-reti nanga kulturu kibri.

Politiki Vertegenwoordiging

Marron politiki vertegenwoordiging ben biksi moro fu den 1990 yari. Dri Marron politiki partijen ben dilek na a 2005 verkiezingen, e gi acht Marron leden fu a Nationale Assemblee nanga dri kabinet posities. Ronnie Brunswijk — owru Jungle Commando leider nanga Ndyuka Marron — ben sweri kon na Visiprezidenti fu Sranan na 16 yulyu 2020, a ferste Marron di hori dati foto, wroko ondro Prezidenti Chan Santokhi. En partij, ABOP, ben leidi a coalitie besturu. Ma politiki vertegenwoordiging na nasronal nivo no klari meki oplossing fu den fundamentele kwesties fu gron-reti, gezonheid zorg, onderwijs infrastructuur na ferwei kondre, nanga mercuriu vervuiling. A gat tra Marron politiki sori-yuru nanga kondre welzijn ini a busi e tan bigi.

Kulturu Libi-Baka & Erfgoed Piki

De wan bigi libi-baka fu interesse na Marron kulturu, bede ini Sranan nanga na diaspora kondre. Winti — a owru religie di ben ferbeidi bijna wan ieuwtenti — kisi momentum fu den 1970 yari nanga e moro moro praktiseri na diaspora kondre na Olanda. 10 Oktober, a nasronal Dey fu den Marron (wan publiki friday fu 1974), e memre nanga seremonie, owru kleding, muziek, dans, nanga kulturu tentoonstellingen na Sranan nanga Olanda. Marron ingi-kapwerk, strip-wevi textiel, nanga kalebasdecoratie e internationaal piki taki wan fu den moro biksi creatieve traditie fu a Afrika diaspora. A Saamaka Maroon Museum na Pikin Slee e dokumenteri a olutu nanga kulturu fu den Saramaka. A Afaka syllabarium — meki na 1910 doro wan Ndyuka man — e kibri taki wan libi skrifi-sisteem.

Houtkap, Chinees Konsesies & Extractieve Industrie

Baka a Binnenlandse Woro klari na 1992, a Sranan besturu gi bigi houtkap konsesies na buitenlandse multinationals — Chinees, Maleis, nanga Indonesisch bedrijven — na owru Marron grontapu, sonder konsultasie nanga Marron besturu. Chinees houtbedrijven, aktief baka wan binnenlansk kapverbod na Sjina, ben wroko direkt na Saramaka nanga Ndyuka gron. Disi ben wan sentrale klagering ini a 2000 Saramaka petitie na a IACtHR. Maski a 2007 beslissing, a konsesie-verlening e tan e go. Moro resent, bigi-skala gowtu operaties — nanga den Newmont Merian mijn na Paramaka grontapu (open na 2016) — bringi formele wroko ma tu brokodey owru besturu strukturen, liba-tran ekonomi, nanga sosraal samenhang ini kondre. A Merian mijn e tyari wroko na honderd Paramaka wroko-sma ma ben tu meki bigi demografische nanga ekonomische brokodey na a Langatabiki presi.

Cultural Heritage

Woodcarving

Maroon men are renowned for intricate woodcarving — canoe paddles, stools, combs, food-preparation objects, and architectural elements. Each piece carries symbolic meaning rooted in West African aesthetics and adapted to forest life over centuries. There is a strong performance and oratorical tradition alongside the visual arts.

Textile Art

Women produce distinctive strip-woven cloths and embroidered garments using vibrant geometric patterns. These textiles are among the most sophisticated in the African diaspora, serving social, spiritual, and aesthetic functions. Life-cycle rituals mark key transitions: the gi pangi ceremony offers a girl a piece of fabric; the gi kamisa presents a boy with adult clothing.

Winti — Traditional Religion

The traditional Surinamese Maroon religion is called Winti — a syncretisation of African religious beliefs and practices, brought primarily by Akan and Fon enslaved peoples. Ancestor veneration is central. Winti has no written sources and no central authority. It was prohibited by law for nearly a century. Since the 1970s it has gained renewed momentum among Maroons in Suriname and the Netherlands.

Governance & Leadership

Each Maroon group (except the Kwinti) is led by a granman (paramount chief), supported by kapiteins (village captains) and basias (heralds and monitors). This governance structure blends African communal models with practices developed over three centuries of autonomous forest life. Society is matrilineal — descent and inheritance pass through the female line.

Tongo

Each Maroon group has its own creole language rooted in West and Central African languages, English, Portuguese, and Dutch. Ndyuka is spoken by the Ndyuka, Aluku, and Paramaka; Saramaccan by the Saramaka and Matawai. These languages remain central to community identity, oral history, and daily life across villages.

Music, Dance & Oral Tradition

Drumming, singing, and dance are central to ceremonies, funerals, and community gatherings. Specific rhythmic patterns carry spiritual significance, communicating with ancestors and deities. The Saramaka and other groups maintain a rich oral tradition, preserving their history of resistance and the stories of their formative years through generations of storytelling.

The Struggle Continues — Present Day

The geographical isolation and cultural distinctiveness that preserved Maroon societies for centuries have, ironically, become major vulnerabilities in the post-independence period. The same rainforest that protected the Maroons now holds an estimated 90 percent of Suriname's natural resources — and that has made their land a target.

Land Rights — Still Unresolved

Despite the 2007 IACtHR landmark ruling, Maroon ancestral lands are still not formally recognised by the Surinamese state. Logging concessions granted to Chinese, Malaysian, Indonesian, and other foreign companies have brought roads, clear-cutting, and destruction directly into Maroon village territories — sometimes with compensation as low as the equivalent of US$6.50 for the destruction of an entire farm. Advocacy has continued through national commissions (1992, 1996, 2000, 2006, 2013) and international courts, but the fundamental legal question of land ownership remains unresolved.

Gowtu Minari — Kansi nanga Dreygisma

Fu a mindri fu den 1980 yari, wan gowtu-rush na ini Sranan busi trek telori telori minari. A moro biksi pisi na Braziliaans garimpeiros, ma wan kwart na Sranan sma — fu de Marron. A moro biksi pisi fu Ndyuka manpikin tide e wroko na pikin-pikin gowtu minari. A moni na nodi; ma a bakaseli na sweri. Mercuriu vervuiling, boskap, nanga a kon fu minari kamp e veranderi liba-ecosysteem nanga kondre libi. Den Marron e feni taki den abi profijt nanga fini skada makandra doro a teki fu sani fu den eigi owru-nen gron.

Waka Go Na Foto & Diaspora

Foto Marron sma waka go na Paramaribo, a ede-foto, nanga na Olanda fu luku moni-kans nanga uku fu suku leri nanga ziekte-swari. Baka a 2012 Volkstelling, 117.567 sma piki taki den na Marron na Sranan — 21.7 prosenti fu a heri bevolking, taki den na a twesi grantapoe etnische grupu. Na Olanda, moro fu ilifendi Marron sma e hori aktieve kondre. A waka go na foto e bringi brokodey fu owru kondre-libi nanga kulturu-armoede, ma tu wan diaspora di e waka e waka moro swaar fu kibri nanga sori Marron eygi fesi.

Politiki Vertegenwoordiging

Dri Marron politiki partei feti na den 2005 verkiezinge, en piki ati Marron lid fu Nasronal Assemblee nanga dri kabinetsplekken taki pisi fu a besturu-koalisie. Na 2020 Ronnie Brunswijk kon Visiprezidenti — a moro hogi foto di wan Marron man ben hori. Ma politiki vertegenwoordiging na nasronal nivo no traduri na oplossing fu a bigi sani fu gron-reti, ziekte-swari, nanga skolu-infrastructuur ini ferwei kondre. A gatu tra politiki vertegenwoordiging nanga kondre welvaart e tan bigi.

Kulturu Libi Baka

Wan bigi libi-baka fu moi na Marron kulturu de, biyin na Sranan nanga tu ini a diaspora. Winti, a owru religie di ben ferbeidi bijna wan ieuwtenti, kisi moro kracht fu den 1970 yari en e waka e waka moro piramoro ini diaspora kondre. 10 Oktober — a Nasronal Dey fu den Marron, wan publiki friday fu 1974 — e firi bigi nanga seremonie, owru dresi, myuzu, dansri, nanga kulturu-sori na heri Sranan nanga na Olanda. Wroko fu dokumenteri tori fu mofu, ingi-kapwerk, wewi ati, nanga tongo e go hay, e draf fu den Marron kondre nanga studie-manpikin.
