Matawai

Also known as: Matuwari · Matawari

De Matawai zijn een Centrale Tak Marron-groep gevestigd langs zijrivieren van de Saramacca in centraal Suriname. Ze zijn de meest gekerstende van de zes Marron-groepen, met een protestantse zendelingsaanwezigheid daterend uit de 18e eeuw. Ze zijn taalkundig en cultureel nauw verwant aan de Saramaka en spreken een variant van het Saramaccaans. Ze tekenden het derde Marron-vredesverdrag in 1767.

History

The Matawai emerged from the same broad movement of resistance and escape that produced the other Maroon groups, though they formed a distinct community somewhat later than the Ndyuka and Saramaka. They are closely related to the Saramaka — culturally, linguistically, and genealogically — and are sometimes described as a branch of the Saramaka who gained independent political recognition.

They signed the third Maroon peace treaty in 1767, four years after the Saramaka. Their territory along tributaries of the Saramacca River is relatively small compared to those of the two largest groups. Protestant missionaries arrived in Matawai territory in the 18th century, and today the Matawai are generally regarded as the most Christianised of the six Maroon groups — a distinction that has both preserved and altered aspects of their traditional culture over two centuries.

American anthropologist Edward C. Green conducted fieldwork among the Matawai between 1970 and 1973, documenting changes then underway in matrilineal kinship structures and traditional spiritual belief systems. His research noted how mission Christianity had begun to interact with and partially displace Winti practices, though elements of traditional religion persisted.

The Interior War of 1986–1992 affected the Matawai, though somewhat less severely than the Ndyuka and Saramaka given their more westward position. Like all Maroon groups, they remain without formal legal recognition of their land rights by the Surinamese state, leaving their territory vulnerable to mining and logging concessions. Matawai men participate in small-scale gold mining, and the community faces the same mercury contamination risks as other Maroon groups in the gold-rich interior.
